Department of Physics and Mathematics

There are a galaxy of reasons to study physics and astrophysics at Hull. There are excellent employment prospects after you graduate and opportunities to get involved in research that tackles unanswered questions.
All of our courses are accredited by the Institute of Physics.



-- A newly published paper by Mikkel Kristensen and Kevin Pimbblet (Hull), along with Samantha Penny (Portsmouth).

This work shows that the environment of a galaxy (where it is located in the large-scale structure of the Universe) is not an important factor in triggering AGN activity in dwarf galaxies. To do this, we looked at nearly 150,000 nearby galaxies from the NASA-Sloan Atlas, a few of which are shown below.

Teaching

Our degree courses encourage you to explore the world from the sub-atomic scale to the cosmological scale under the guidance of internationally recognised experts.

The early part of your degree will give you grounding in core subjects such as quantum physics, relativity, particle and nuclear physics, solid state physics, light, fields and matter. Later, you'll cover specialist topics including galaxy formation, nanophysics, superconductivity and general relativity.

Research

Our staff's research interests range from the very small (quantum theory) to the very large (galaxy evolution). And between those points we carry out specialist work in condensed matter physics, organophotonics, nanophysics and light-matter interactions.
