Did you know?

A few key facts about the Department

Moon rocks were studied in the Department: one of the few institutions outside the USA

Liquid crystals were developed in the University, kick-starting the revolution in displays

The bone density scanner, a key diagnostic tool for osteoporosis was developed in the Department

The Department developed  the world's first X-ray laser was, which held the record for shortest wavelength for 14 years

Prof. Ronald Shaw, who came to Hull in Mathematical Physics right after his PhD in Cambridge, is one of the authors of the Yang-Mills-Shaw theory which is central to particle physics

The Department leads a £5.6 Million OfS grant to deliver fantastic work experience opportunities for physics students and allows access to an extensive range of work based learning

We run 100+ events per year, spanning a range of widening participation, diversity in STEM, teacher CPD, and curriculum-enhancing support across two Local Authorities

We ran an unprecedented simulation of the Universe spanning its first 8 billion years of existence. We develop essential tools for simulations to probe the formation of elements in the Universe
